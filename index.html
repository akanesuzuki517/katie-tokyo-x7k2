Taxi to Nakameguro · ~10 min

Yōshoku is Japan's own take on Western food — dishes that arrived in the late 1800s and got quietly reinvented into comfort food. Kougai Ken is the Nakameguro branch of a beloved Hiroo original, set in a warm, slightly nostalgic room of whites and browns.

The thing to order is the omu-hayashi: a fluffy, just-set omelette over ketchup-y rice, with a little skillet of demi-glace poured tableside. The homemade hamburg steak is the other classic, and there's a proper Basque cheesecake if we have room.

Good to know

It's a small, popular spot (a bit TikTok-famous) — but we have a reservation, so no queue for us. Each person orders a main plus a drink.

Walk to Don Quijote · ~6 min

Donki is the chaotic, ceiling-high discount store every visitor ends up loving — beauty, snacks, gadgets, souvenirs, all in one maze. Tax-free for tourists, and open 24 hours. A fun, low-stakes stop to hunt for things to bring home.

Crowd favorites

Sheet masks (Lululun), Kit Kats in Japan-only flavors, Royce' / Donki-exclusive cheese snacks, Roihi-Tsuboko patches, and DHC vitamins. Bring your passport for tax-free.

Walk to HIVE Preloved · ~3 min

A curated showroom of vintage designer bags — known for unique pieces you won't see everywhere: Fendi baguettes, Dior saddles, and the like. They keep a coat/bag drop area and let you browse at your own pace, with help when you want it. Staff speak perfect English.

No pressure

We've got a 15-minute private slot. Since we're hitting several vintage shops after this, treat this as a quick scan — only worth lingering if something here is genuinely your dream piece.

Out by 13:30 · taxi to Omotesando · ~15–20 min

The Jingumae backstreets are Tokyo's vintage-luxury heartland. Two standouts, almost next door to each other:

QOO — an exceptional thrift-meets-luxury shop (4.9★ from thousands): vintage Cartier and Rolex watches, designer bags, jewelry across every price point. They sometimes limit entry, so there can be a short queue.

AMORE Vintage Aoyama — the place for vintage Chanel and Fendi in beautiful condition, with warm, English-speaking staff who'll walk you through a bag's history. There are a few AMORE outposts within a block.

Pace

This is the heart of the day — take your time. Tax-free counters at both; keep your passport handy.
